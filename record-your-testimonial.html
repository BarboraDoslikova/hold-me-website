Our Goal

The Caregiver Outreach campaign associated with the feature film Hold Me is intended to bring together a variety of testimonials from caregivers all over the world. These testimonials will pass along the wisdom and experiences of those who work closely with the dying.

The feature film Hold Me centers on the caregiving experiences of a woman named Hannah whose job it is to hold and console people that are entering the process of physician assisted dying. If you would like to watch the film before providing your testimonial, a private link to see it free of charge is available here:

As you can see, the film does not take a firm position on the practice of physician assisted death, it merely allows the issue to underscore how emotionally overwhelming the responsibilities of caregiving at the end of life can be. It is precisely for this reason that we are creating the online database of real world caregiver testimonials–so that those who are facing loss or who are feeling overwhelmed by their responsibilities will have a place of catharsis and reflection online.

Thank you very much for your time, consideration and for the extremely important work that you have dedicated your life to. It is our hope that your experiences and those of other caregivers like you can help empower and educate the world.

Questions:

1. What is a valuable story or account you can share about someone you have accompanied?

2. In the film Hold Me, Hannah struggles with talking about her grief. What advice would you offer people struggling with expressing or coping with their feelings?

3. How do you manage the emotional toll that working with the dying can bring?

4. What do you feel people want from a caregiver at the end of life?

5. What has working with the dying taught you about life?
